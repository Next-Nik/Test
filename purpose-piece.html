How you're built
to contribute.

You already have a pattern — the way you show up when something needs doing.

You've been living it for years. The role you instinctively move into. The territory that holds your attention. The way things go better when you're in your natural position and worse when you're not.

That pattern isn't random. It's how you naturally contribute when something meaningful is happening.

Most people never have a name for it.

Purpose Piece gives you one.

A different kind of assessment.

Many assessments describe who you are, what you're good at, or what career might suit you.

Purpose Piece focuses on something different: how you're built to contribute — and where that contribution belongs.

The difference is behavioral. Five questions, each anchored to something you actually did — not what you prefer, not what you value in the abstract, but what you concretely did when it mattered. The pattern emerges from what you've already lived. The archetype follows from the pattern.

Three lenses. One intersection.

Archetype

The specific role you naturally play in any living system. Not your personality. The functional shape you take when things need to get done.

Domain

The territory where that role belongs in the larger work. Seven areas of collective life, each with its own question it's trying to answer.

Scale

The bandwidth at which you naturally operate. Not ambition. Not status. The scope where you can act with full presence and genuine effectiveness.

Archetype + Domain + Scale = Your Purpose Piece.

A Connector in Society at Local scale. A Guardian in Nature at Bioregional scale. An Architect in Vision at Civilisational scale. Same framework. Entirely different people. Entirely different work.

The combination is what locates you. Not the archetype alone. Not the domain alone. The intersection of all three.

What the output feels like.

First you receive a behavioral mirror — a description of the pattern the system observed in how you actually move. No labels yet. Just: here is what was noticed.

Then the archetype arrives. Not assigned. Recognized. As a consequence of everything that came before.

The test isn't "that's accurate." It's "oh."

That moment — the one where a pattern you've been living finally has a name — is the product. Everything else is in service of it.
