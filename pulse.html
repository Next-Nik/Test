Check your pulse.

Your daily awareness practice — across every domain of your life.

Having awareness of your current state — and tracking that over time — allows you to navigate your life from a clearer picture than most people ever access.

They know some things are working. They sense others aren't. But that information gets lost in the noise of day to day life.

Taking a moment every day to drop in with yourself can change your life. The millions of microdecisions you make daily start to shift from this place of basic awareness.

Without that clear picture, it's hard to know where to focus. With it, you can step towards your potential.

Pulse gives you that moment.

No prerequisites.

Pulse works on its own. You don't need to have done Orienteering first. You don't need a map before you start checking in. The daily practice of honest self-awareness is valuable at any stage — before the map, during it, after it.

If you have done Orienteering, Pulse keeps that map current. If you haven't, Pulse starts building one for you, one honest check-in at a time.

Three rhythms. One practice.

Daily

A quick check across all seven domains. Where are you today, honestly? Not a performance review — a reading. Two minutes. Keeps the signal honest between weekly check-ins.

Weekly

A brief reflection. What changed? Where did you stall or move? Not a planning session — a recalibration. Keeping the heading clear.

Quarterly Reset

The full reassessment. All seven domains re-rated honestly. Focus domains chosen for the next 90 days. Patterns made visible across time. It marks evolution — it shows you who you were 90 days ago and who you are now.

What compounds over time.

The daily practice alone doesn't produce transformation. What it produces is data — honest, self-sourced data about how your life is actually moving.

Over a quarter, patterns become visible. Over a year, the evolution becomes undeniable. The domains that have genuinely shifted. The ones that keep falling short despite attention. The gap between where you thought you were and where you actually are.

That kind of self-knowledge is rare. Not because it's complicated — but because almost nothing encourages you to stay honest with yourself consistently over time.

Pulse is the practice that makes it possible.

Not optimisation. Orientation.

The moment scores become something to perform well on, the tool has inverted. Scores are information, not achievements. A 4 honestly placed is more valuable than a 7 that performs adequacy.

There is no external 10/10 to measure yourself against. The picture of what 10/10 looks like in each domain is yours — built from your life, your context. This eliminates comparison and creates honest, personal data that actually means something.
